About Megan Matthews

Experiential Designer & Contemporary Media Artist

Hailing from Colorado, Megan seeks to redefine how people engage with technology, art, and the people around her. Using digital art and creative tech as her primary mediums, she hopes to blur the line between fantasy and reality in order to create an elevated sense of reality. She employs many different techniques in order to achieve this sense of elevated reality including projection mapping, virtual and augmented reality, programming, fabrication, animation, and more.

Redesign Notes

From Bad to Better

Overall

I changed basically my entire website from the previous version to this one. I felt that I had cluttered the previous website up with so much html that it made more sense to "remake" it almost from scratch in order to cleanly incorporate Bootstraps.

Home Page

My homepage used to be black and purple, with a sparkling gif in the background. Everything was centered because that seemed to be the best stylistic choice I could make using HTML. The buttons were clean but I like this design much better. Its not all thrown into the center of the screen, it looks cleaner and having things balanced with both the menu and the picture is ideal. Additionally I was able to add a navbar and a row of social media icon/links which all really helped improve the look as well.

About Page

I changed so much on my about page from it being basically a carbon copy of my home page to now having vital information, a semi-decent headshot, and really cool informative assets. I loved putting in the progress bars and think they really add a touch to my site. Additionally I used some icons to highlight interests which spices it up too.

Portfolio Page

Previously, on my portfolio page I had just thrown in a screen shot of a gallery on my old website because I was intimidated trying to figure out how to upload a bunch of project assets as a gallery. I found a good layout here and was able to upload a bunch. This section definitely still needs the most work as I want to be able to have people be able to dive deeper than just my still images (most of my work is video based).

Contact Page

My contact page probably had the least amount of changing, but still looks way different. Like all the other pages I threw in icons, and even a little form to send emails through my site.

Inspiring Websites Page

We didn't have this in our previous website but I wanted to note that I was able to accomplish this format by using a resume builder with bootsrap. I think it worked fairly well, although I might later find a different way to embed the links.
